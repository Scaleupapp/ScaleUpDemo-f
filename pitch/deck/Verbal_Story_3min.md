# ScaleUp — 3-Minute Verbal Story Arc

*This is the opening monologue before you touch the deck. Memorize the structure, not the words. Deliver conversationally, not like a script.*

---

## [0:00 — 0:30] THE GAP

"India produces 1.1 crore graduates every year. I've spent 15 years hiring them — at Deloitte, at Target, at GE. And the same thing kept happening: brilliant people who couldn't show me they were ready.

Not because they didn't study. They studied plenty. But there was always this gap between what they knew and what they could prove. And nobody was bridging it."

## [0:30 — 1:00] THE FAILED FIX

"Edtech tried. ₹22,000 crore at BYJU's. 2,148 startups in 5 years. All content-first. All dead or dying.

And here's why: content isn't the problem anymore. YouTube, ChatGPT, NotebookLM — content is free and infinite. But free content created a new problem. A 22-year-old searching 'how to become a product manager' gets 50,000 results and zero clarity. No one knows what's reliable. No one knows what actually works. And critically — no one measures whether you actually became job-ready."

## [1:00 — 1:45] WHAT WE BUILT

"That's what ScaleUp does differently. We're India's first outcome-based learning platform.

Three things, together, that nobody else does:

First — curated, bite-sized content from verified practitioners. Not 3-hour lectures. 15-minute sessions from people who've actually done the job. We're building the platform that educational creators aspire to be on.

Second — a Content-to-Outcomes loop. We call it C2O. Every piece of content connects to an AI-powered assessment. Every assessment shows you your gaps. Every gap triggers the right next step. Automatically. And the loop gets smarter every cycle.

Third — the outcome data this loop generates becomes our moat. In 18 months, we'll know which content led to which skill improvement, which preparation pattern led to which job offer, at which company. That's data Scaler can't buy at ₹2.5 lakh per user and Physics Wallah can't generate from exam cram."

## [1:45 — 2:15] WHY NOW + WHY US

"Why now? Because the market just cleared. BYJU's is gone. Unacademy was acquired by upGrad last month. 2,148 competitors died. But on the other side — Physics Wallah IPO'd at $5.2B, proving survivors win. Peak XV just raised $1.3B with AI as top priority. The capital is ready for the right AI-native edtech bet.

Why us? I built products at Deloitte, Target, GE, Vodafone for 15 years — I saw this gap from the employer side. My co-founder Pratiksha drove growth at ANAROCK and Merkle Sokrati — she knows how to acquire and retain users at scale. Together, we've already built full iOS and Android apps, a working C2O loop with AI mock interviews, and production analytics — bootstrapped, with 2 part-time devs."

## [2:15 — 2:45] THE ASK

"We're live with PM, AI/coding, and entrepreneurship. Competitive exams in 3 months. 100 beta testers from IIT-B and DJ Sanghvi.

We're raising ₹4 to 6 crore on a SAFE at ₹30 crore cap. 12 months of runway to hire 16 curated creators, prove the C2O loop generates real outcome data, and hit 5,000 paying subscribers.

We're not looking for just capital. We're looking for 2-3 strategic partners who bring distribution, creator networks, or domain expertise."

## [2:45 — 3:00] THE CLOSE

"India doesn't need another content library. India needs someone to close the loop between learning and outcomes. That's what we're building.

Let me show you the product."

*[Transition to live demo of C2O loop]*

## DELIVERY NOTES

- **Pace:** Conversational, not rushed. Pause after "₹22,000 crore at BYJU's" — let it land.
- **Eye contact:** Look at the person, not the slides, for the first 2 minutes. Slides are backup.
- **Hands:** When you say "three things," hold up 1-2-3 fingers. Physical anchoring.
- **Energy shift:** Start measured and serious (the gap). Get progressively energized as you move to "what we built." Peak energy at "that's data Scaler can't buy."
- **The close:** Slow down for "India doesn't need another content library." Make it a statement, not a sell.
- **Transition to demo:** Don't wait for applause. Just say "let me show you" and pull up the phone/screen.
